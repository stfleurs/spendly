The Real Cost of Subscription Creep

Most people significantly underestimate how much they spend on subscriptions. A $10 monthly charge feels insignificant, but across multiple services — streaming, cloud storage, fitness apps, software, boxes — the total can easily reach hundreds of dollars per month.

This phenomenon is called "subscription creep": small, recurring charges that fly under the radar because they're automatic. Unlike one-time purchases, subscriptions never end unless you cancel them. A $15/month subscription costs $180 per year and $900 over five years. Your calculator shows the real math.

How to Audit Your Subscriptions

  • Review your bank and credit card statements for recurring charges. Look for charges you don't recognize.
  • Ask yourself: Am I using this regularly? If you haven't used a service in the past 30 days, cancel it.
  • Look for duplicates. Many people subscribe to multiple streaming services and only use one or two.
  • Check for annual billing discounts. Many services offer 15-25% off if you switch from monthly to annual billing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many subscriptions does the average person have?

Studies show the average person has 4-6 active subscriptions and underestimates their total monthly cost by 2-3x. Streaming services, cloud storage, and software are the most common categories.

What subscriptions should I cancel first?

Start with services you haven't used in the past month. Next, look for overlapping services (multiple streaming platforms, multiple cloud storage services). Finally, consider whether you can replace paid services with free alternatives.

How can I prevent subscription creep?

Schedule a quarterly subscription audit. Set a calendar reminder every 3 months to review all recurring charges. Using an app like Receet Pro automates this — it tracks subscriptions continuously so you always know what's coming out of your account.
